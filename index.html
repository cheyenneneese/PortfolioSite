About me

I earned my Master of Public Health (MPH) in Epidemiology with a focus on data analysis from the University of Texas Health Science Center School of Public Health. Prior to that, I completed my undergraduate degree in Nutritional Science at The University of Texas. During my master's program, I gained hands-on experience through internships with Foundation Communities and The Arc of the Capital Area, where I contributed to data analysis, program evaluation, and collaborated on projects aimed at improving community health and well-being. After completing my MPH, I had the unique opportunity to live and work in the Middle East, where I immersed myself in Arabic language studies and developed cross-cultural communication skills. As a Language Coordinator/Coach, I supported our director and gained valuable insights into diverse cultures, values, and perspectives while working in Lebanon and Jordan. I have since returned to Texas and am currently working as an analyst at Mathematica, where I continue to apply my expertise in data analysis and public health to drive impactful projects..
